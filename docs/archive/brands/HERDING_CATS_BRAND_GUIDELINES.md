## ğŸ—£ï¸ **VOICE & TONE**

### **Brand Voice Attributes**

**1. VERBOSE & ARTICULATE**
We use more words than necessary. Intentionally. It's part of the charm.

**BAD:** "We'll pick you up and visit wineries."  
**GOOD:** "We shall collect your party from the designated coordinates and commence a carefully orchestrated progression through Walla Walla's most distinguished viticultural establishments."

**2. DRY WIT**
Humor delivered with a straight face. Think British comedy, not slapstick.

**BAD:** "Wine tours are hard when everyone wants something different! LOL"  
**GOOD:** "Achieving consensus among individuals with disparate palate preferences proves the Sisyphean nature of our endeavor. We persist regardless."

**3. WINE-EDUCATED**
We demonstrate real wine knowledge. This isn't a gimmick covering ignorance.

**BAD:** "We'll taste some reds and whites!"  
**GOOD:** "Our progression begins with aromatic whitesâ€”Viognier, perhaps, or a well-structured Roussanneâ€”before advancing toward Syrah and the valley's celebrated Cabernet blends."

**4. FELINE METAPHORS (SUBTLE)**
Cat references are woven in naturally, never forced.

**BAD:** "Our purr-fect tours are paw-some!"  
**GOOD:** "Much like our feline namesakes, we operate with independence of spirit yet respond (eventually) to direction."

**5. SELF-AWARE**
We acknowledge the inherent absurdity of our name and task.

**BAD:** "We're the best at herding cats!"  
**GOOD:** "The task remains, technically speaking, impossible. Our success rate merely suggests otherwise."
